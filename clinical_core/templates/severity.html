Clinical Severity Guide

Understanding symptom-based indicators and dietary mapping.

Why this Dataset is Superior

Unlike traditional datasets that focus solely on lab numbers, our early-stage dataset prioritizes clinical symptoms.

Polyuria & Polydipsia

Excessive urination and thirst are primary "Red Flag" indicators. When blood sugar is high, kidneys work overtime to filter it, leading to dehydration and intense thirst.

Sudden Weight Loss

When the body can't use glucose for energy due to insulin issues, it starts burning fat and muscle for fuel, causing rapid, unexplained weight loss.

Delayed Healing

High blood sugar levels over time can affect blood flow and cause nerve damage, which impairs the body's ability to heal wounds and fight infections.

Symptom-Based Severity Mapping

Our AI categorizes risk into 4 distinct levels to provide more precise dietary intelligence.

Level 0: Negative Class

Status: No significant indicators detected.

Strategy: Normal balanced diet. Focus on maintenance and routine annual screenings.

Level 1: Early Awareness

Status: Positive prediction with 1-2 mild symptoms.

Strategy: Focus on high fiber and low-glycemic fruits. Moderate carbohydrate control.

Level 2: High Alert

Status: Positive prediction with 3-4 significant symptoms.

Strategy: Strict Low-Carb (Keto-friendly) approach. Eliminate all refined flours and sugars immediately.

Level 3: Clinical Intervention

Status: Positive prediction with 5+ symptoms or extreme indicators.

Strategy: Medical-grade diet plan. Focus on high hydration, calorie deficit, and immediate endocrinologist consultation.
